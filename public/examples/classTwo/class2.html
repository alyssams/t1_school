HOW HELVETICA CONQUERED THE WORLD WTH ITS COOL.

The digital version of Helvetica that everyone knows and uses today is quite different from the typeface's pre-digital design from 1957.
Originally released as Neue Haas Grotesk, many of the features that made it a Modernist favorite have been lost in translation over the years from one typesetting technology to the next.

"The font also manages to convey honesty ad invite trust."

  1. The text styles of Helvetica come equipped with a set of tabular numerals and related symbols.
  2. Neue Haas Grotesk includes features for numeric typography, including proper fractions, separate numerators and denominators.
  3. With language support for Afrikaans, Basque, Breton, Catalan, Croatian, Czech, Danish, Dutch, English, Estonian, Finnish.
  • The Helvetica obliques that come installed on every Mac today were generated mechanically by skewing regular upright forms 12.
  • A number of alternate forms were offered by request for the original Neue Haas Grotesk.
  • When all-caps styling is applied, numerals and punctuation marks will be replaced with variants that align better with the capital letters.
